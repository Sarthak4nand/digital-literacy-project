# Identity Theft – A Case Study

## What is it?
Identity theft is when someone steals your personal information 
like your name, Aadhar number, PAN card details or bank info 
and uses it to pretend to be you — usually to take loans, 
make purchases or open accounts in your name without you knowing.

## How it happened – A Real Scenario

My neighbour's son Rahul got a call in his second year of college 
from someone claiming to be from his bank's KYC department. The 
caller said his account would be blocked in 24 hours if he didn't 
complete his KYC update immediately.

The caller sent him a link that looked exactly like his bank's 
official website. Rahul filled in his full name, date of birth, 
Aadhar number, PAN number and account details thinking it was 
the real bank website.

Three days later Rahul got a message saying a personal loan of 
Rs. 45,000 had been approved in his name. He had never applied 
for any loan. Someone had used his details to apply for it at 
a different bank entirely.

It took Rahul's family over four months of running between banks 
and police stations to get it sorted. His CIBIL score was badly 
affected and he couldn't even open a new account easily during 
that time.

## Who gets targeted?
College students and young adults who are new to banking and 
don't know what KYC actually looks like. Also people who share 
personal documents carelessly on WhatsApp or email.

## What are the consequences?
- Loans and credit cards taken out in your name without consent
- Damaged credit score making future loans very difficult
- Months of legal and bank visits to resolve the issue
- Mental stress and loss of trust in online services
- In some cases criminal records can also get linked to your identity

## The one thing to always remember
No bank ever asks for your Aadhar, PAN or full account details 
over a call or through a link. If someone does — hang up immediately 
and call your bank's official helpline directly.

Source: Based on patterns reported on cybercrime.gov.in and 
RBI guidelines on KYC fraud awareness (2024)
