## ✅ DO's — Responsible Social Media Use

| # | Do This | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Think before you post** — ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or future employer saw this?" | Your digital footprint is permanent. What you post today can affect opportunities years from now. |
| 2 | **Keep your profiles professional** — use a clear, decent profile photo and a real name on platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub. | Employers and internship coordinators check social media before hiring. A professional profile builds trust. |
| 3 | **Use privacy settings** — set personal accounts (Instagram, Facebook) to private so only trusted people can see your content. | Limiting your public audience protects you from strangers, scammers, and unwanted attention. |
| 4 | **Give credit when sharing** — always mention the original creator or source when you share someone else's work, ideas, or images. | Sharing without credit is plagiarism online. It is unethical and can damage your reputation. |
| 5 | **Report and block harmful content** — if you see cyberbullying, fake news, or harassment, use the platform's report feature. | Reporting helps keep online spaces safer for everyone, including yourself. |
| 6 | **Log out of shared devices** — always log out of your accounts when using college computer labs, borrowed laptops, or public devices. | Leaving accounts open on shared devices exposes your personal data to misuse. |
| 7 | **Verify news before sharing** — check if a piece of news is from a credible source before sharing it with others. | Spreading misinformation — even accidentally — harms others and damages your credibility. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts — What to Avoid

| # | Never Do This | Why It Is Risky |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Never share personal information publicly** — avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID, or Aadhaar details on any public platform. | This information can be used by scammers and identity thieves to impersonate or harm you. |
| 2 | **Never post in anger or frustration** — do not rant about teachers, classmates, or your college on public social media. | Emotional posts are easily screenshot and shared. They have led to real cases of disciplinary action and withdrawn job offers. |
| 3 | **Never share your OTP, passwords, or bank details** — no legitimate platform or person will ever ask for these online. | Sharing this information — even with someone you trust — can result in financial fraud or account takeover. |
| 4 | **Never engage with or reply to trolls and cyberbullies** — do not argue back or retaliate publicly. | Engaging gives bullies attention and escalates the situation. Block, report, and move on. |
| 5 | **Never accept friend/follow requests from strangers** — especially those with incomplete profiles or no mutual connections. | Fake accounts are used to gather personal data, spread malware, or conduct social engineering attacks. |
| 6 | **Never click on suspicious links shared via DMs or comments** — even if they appear to come from a friend's account. | Accounts get hacked. A link from a compromised account can install malware or steal your login credentials. |
| 7 | **Never use social media during exams, vivas, or important deadlines** — it is a major source of distraction and procrastination. | Studies show social media reduces concentration and productivity when used during study periods. |

## 🚨 If Something Goes Wrong Online

- **Cyberbullying or online harassment** → Report to the platform + National Cyber Crime Portal: [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- **Financial fraud or UPI scam** → Call Cyber Crime Helpline: **1930** (24x7, Government of India)
- **Account hacked** → Change password immediately + enable two-factor authentication + report to platform
- **Identity theft** → File a complaint at [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) immediately
